Tuning a harp doesn't have to feel like a puzzle.

If you've ever stared at all those strings and wondered where to even begin, Harpie was made for you. Whether you just unwrapped your first lap harp, you're helping your child tune before their lesson, or you're a seasoned Celtic harpist who just wants something that works — Harpie makes the whole process calm, clear, and actually kind of fun.

Most tuners are built with guitarists in mind. They're cluttered, confusing, and assume you already know what you're doing. Harpie is different. It was designed from the ground up for harp players, with the strings, ranges, and quirks of real harps in mind.

— HOW IT WORKS —

Open the app, pick your harp type, and pluck a string. Harpie listens, identifies the pitch automatically, and shows you exactly how close you are to being in tune. The animated arc gauge gives you a beautiful, at-a-glance read — swing it left or right and you know instantly whether to tighten or loosen. No music theory required.

— FEATURES —

• Auto pitch detection — just pluck and let Harpie listen; no need to select strings manually
• Manual string selection — browse your harp's full layout and tune any string in any order
• Animated arc pitch gauge — a smooth, satisfying visual that shows how sharp or flat you are in real time
• Reference tones — coming soon: hear the target pitch before you pluck (great for beginners training their ear)
• Quiet environment mode — works best in a calm room, just like a real tuning session

— SUPPORTED HARPS —

Harpie knows the full range of each instrument, so it only listens for notes that actually belong on your harp:

• Lap Harp (15 strings, C4–C6) — perfect for beginners and younger players
• Lever / Celtic Harp (34 strings, A1–F6) — the most popular choice for folk and contemporary harpists
• Pedal Harp (47 strings, C1–G7) — full concert range for classical players

Not sure which type you have? The in-app guide helps you identify your instrument in seconds.

— BUILT FOR REAL LIFE —

Harpie is designed to fit into the actual moments when you need to tune — before a lesson, after moving your harp, or when a string just sounds a little off. It launches fast, responds immediately, and gets out of your way so you can start playing.

Parents: if your child is learning harp, Harpie is gentle enough for them to use on their own. The interface is clean, the visuals are intuitive, and there's no technical jargon to wade through.

— COMING SOON —

Harpie is actively being developed. Reference tones and additional features are on the way. Download now to be ready the moment it launches — and share your feedback to help shape what comes next.

Ready to tune? Harpie is here.
